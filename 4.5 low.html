Homogeneous Equation & Lines

The Origin Pair: \(ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0\)

This second-degree equation always represents a pair of straight lines passing through the origin (0,0).

Joint Equation: \[ ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \]

Understanding Homogeneous Equations

What is a Homogeneous Equation?

A homogeneous equation is one where all terms have the same degree. In our case, all terms are of degree 2: \(x^2\), \(xy\), and \(y^2\). This ensures that if (x,y) is a solution, then (kx,ky) is also a solution for any constant k.

Why Do They Represent Lines Through the Origin?

Since all terms have degree 2, if (x,y) satisfies the equation, then (kx,ky) also satisfies it. This means the equation is scale-invariant, which geometrically means all solution points lie on straight lines passing through the origin.

Visual Representation

The equation \(ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0\) can be factored into two linear equations: \((m_1x - y)(m_2x - y) = 0\), representing two lines \(y = m_1x\) and \(y = m_2x\) passing through the origin.

The slopes \(m_1\) and \(m_2\) can be found using the quadratic formula applied to the equation in terms of \(m = y/x\).

Two Lines Through Origin

Key Properties

‚Ä¢ The product of slopes \(m_1 \cdot m_2 = \frac{a}{b}\)

‚Ä¢ The sum of slopes \(m_1 + m_2 = -\frac{2h}{b}\)

‚Ä¢ For real lines to exist: \(h^2 - ab \ge 0\)

Angle Between the Lines

Angle Formula: \(\tan\theta = \left| \frac{2 \sqrt{h^2 - ab}}{a + b} \right|\)

The acute angle (\(\theta\)) between the two lines can be found directly from the coefficients a, h, b of the equation.

Angle Between Lines Formula: \[ \tan\theta = \left| \frac{2 \sqrt{h^2 - ab}}{a + b} \right| \]

Understanding the Angle Between Lines

Derivation of the Angle Formula

The angle between two lines with slopes \(m_1\) and \(m_2\) is given by:

\[ \tan\theta = \left| \frac{m_1 - m_2}{1 + m_1 m_2} \right| \]

For the homogeneous equation \(ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0\), the slopes satisfy:

\[ m_1 + m_2 = -\frac{2h}{b}, \quad m_1 m_2 = \frac{a}{b} \]

Substituting these into the angle formula gives us the result:

\[ \tan\theta = \left| \frac{2 \sqrt{h^2 - ab}}{a + b} \right| \]

Special Cases

Perpendicular Lines: When \(a + b = 0\), the denominator becomes zero, making \(\tan\theta\) undefined, which corresponds to \(\theta = 90^\circ\).

Coincident Lines: When \(h^2 - ab = 0\), the numerator becomes zero, giving \(\theta = 0^\circ\).

Real Lines Condition: For real lines to exist, we need \(h^2 - ab \ge 0\).

Visualizing the Angle

The angle between two lines is the smaller angle at their intersection point. For lines through the origin, this is simply the angle between their direction vectors.

Find \(\theta\) for \(10x^2 - 23xy - 5y^2 = 0\). (\(a=10, b=-5, h=-23/2\))

Example 2 ‚Äî Obtuse and Acute Angles
Find the angle \(\theta\) between \(2x^2 + 3xy - 5y^2 = 0\). (\(a=2, b=-5, h=3/2\))
Step 1: Identify: \(a=2, b=-5, h = \frac{3}{2}\).
Step 2: Substitute: \(\tan\theta = \left| \frac{2 \sqrt{\frac{9}{4} + 10}}{2 - 5} \right| = \left| \frac{2 \cdot \frac{7}{2}}{-3} \right| = \left| -\frac{7}{3} \right|\).
Step 3: \(\theta_{acute} = \arctan(\frac{7}{3}) \approx 66^\circ 48'\).
Answer: The acute angle is \(66^\circ 48'\). The obtuse angle is \(180^\circ - 66^\circ 48' = 113^\circ 12'\).

Example 3 ‚Äî Angle Involving a Variable (\(\alpha\))
Find \(\theta\) for \(x^2 - 2xy \sec\alpha + y^2 = 0\). (Use identity \(\sec^2\alpha - 1 = \tan^2\alpha\))
Step 1: Identify: \(a=1, b=1, h=-\sec\alpha\).
Step 2: Substitute: \(\tan\theta = \left| \frac{2 \sqrt{(-\sec\alpha)^2 - (1)(1)}}{1 + 1} \right|\).
Step 3: Simplify: \(\tan\theta = \frac{2 \sqrt{\sec^2\alpha - 1}}{2} = \sqrt{\tan^2\alpha} = |\tan\alpha|\).
Answer: \(\theta = \alpha\) (if \(\alpha\) is acute).

Perpendicular Joint Equation

The Perpendicular Flip: \(bx^2 - 2hxy + ay^2 = 0\)

The joint equation of lines perpendicular to \(ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0\) is a simple swap of a and b and a sign change of the 2h term.

Perpendicular Joint Equation: \[ bx^2 - 2hxy + ay^2 = 0 \]

Understanding Perpendicular Lines

Why This Transformation Works

If two lines have slopes \(m_1\) and \(m_2\), then lines perpendicular to them will have slopes \(-\frac{1}{m_1}\) and \(-\frac{1}{m_2}\).

For the homogeneous equation \(ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0\), the product of slopes is \(m_1 m_2 = \frac{a}{b}\).

For perpendicular lines, the product of slopes should be \(-1\), which happens when we swap a and b and change the sign of h.

Geometric Interpretation

Perpendicular lines form right angles (90¬∞) with the original lines. If you rotate the original lines by 90¬∞, you get the perpendicular lines.

The transformation \(x \rightarrow y\) and \(y \rightarrow -x\) effectively rotates the coordinate system by 90¬∞, which is why it gives us the perpendicular joint equation.

Example 1 ‚Äî General Perpendicular Form
Find the joint equation perpendicular to \(ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0\).
Step 1: Substitute \(x \rightarrow y\) and \(y \rightarrow -x\) into the original equation.
Step 2: \(a(y)^2 + 2h(y)(-x) + b(-x)^2 = 0\).
Step 3: \(ay^2 - 2hxy + bx^2 = 0\).
Answer: \(bx^2 - 2hxy + ay^2 = 0\). (This confirms the swap rule).
üß¶
Example 2 ‚Äî Simple Perpendicular Form
Find the joint equation perpendicular to \(10x^2 - 23xy - 5y^2 = 0\).
Step 1: Original: \(a=10, b=-5, 2h=-23\).
Step 2: Apply the swap: \(b \rightarrow -5, a \rightarrow 10\).
Step 3: Change \(xy\) sign: \(-23xy \rightarrow +23xy\).
Step 4: Construct: \(-5x^2 + 23xy + 10y^2 = 0\).
Answer: \(5x^2 - 23xy - 10y^2 = 0\).
üß≠
Example 3 ‚Äî Perpendicular to \(x^2 - 2xy \tan\alpha - y^2 = 0\)
Since \(a+b=0\) (\(1 + (-1) = 0\)), the original lines are already perpendicular.
Step 1: Original: \(a=1, b=-1, 2h=-2\tan\alpha\).
Step 2: Apply the swap: \(b \rightarrow -1, a \rightarrow 1\).
Step 3: Change \(xy\) sign: \(-2xy \tan\alpha \rightarrow +2xy \tan\alpha\).
Step 4: Construct: \(-x^2 + 2xy \tan\alpha + y^2 = 0\).
Answer: \(x^2 - 2xy \tan\alpha - y^2 = 0\) (The original equation is the same as the perpendicular one).

Area Bounded by Lines

Area Bounded by \(L_1, L_2\) and \(L_3\)

To find the area of the triangle formed by the homogeneous pair (\(L_1, L_2\)) and a third line (\(L_3\)), you need the three vertices.

Area Formula: \[ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \left| x_A(y_B - y_C) + x_B(y_C - y_A) + x_C(y_A - y_B) \right| \]

Understanding Area Calculations

The Area Formula

The formula for the area of a triangle with vertices \((x_A, y_A), (x_B, y_B), (x_C, y_C)\) is:

\[ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \left| x_A(y_B - y_C) + x_B(y_C - y_A) + x_C(y_A - y_B) \right| \]

This formula works for any triangle, regardless of its orientation. The absolute value ensures the area is always positive.

Special Case: One Vertex at Origin

When one vertex is at the origin (0,0), the formula simplifies to:

\[ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \left| x_B y_C - x_C y_B \right| \]

This is because \(x_A = 0\) and \(y_A = 0\), so many terms in the general formula become zero.

Visualizing the Triangle

The triangle is formed by:

‚Ä¢ Vertex A: The intersection of \(L_1\) and \(L_2\) (always the origin for homogeneous equations)

‚Ä¢ Vertex B: The intersection of \(L_1\) and \(L_3\)

‚Ä¢ Vertex C: The intersection of \(L_2\) and \(L_3\)

Example ‚Äî Area Calculation
Area bounded by \(10x^2 - xy - 21y^2 = 0\) and \(x + y + 1 = 0\).
Step 1: Factor: \(10x^2 - xy - 21y^2 = (2x - 3y)(5x + 7y) = 0\).
Step 2: Vertices: \(A(0, 0)\).
Step 3: \(L_1 \cap L_3\): Solve \(2x - 3y = 0\) and \(x = -y - 1\) to get \(B(-\frac{3}{5}, -\frac{2}{5})\).
Step 4: \(L_2 \cap L_3\): Solve \(5x + 7y = 0\) and \(x = -y - 1\) to get \(C(\frac{7}{2}, -\frac{9}{2})\).
Step 5: Use Area formula (simplified for \(A(0,0)\)): \(\frac{1}{2} \left| x_B y_C - x_C y_B \right|\).
Step 6: Area $= \frac{1}{2} \left| (-\frac{3}{5})(-\frac{9}{2}) - (\frac{7}{2})(-\frac{2}{5}) \right| = \frac{1}{2} \left| \frac{27}{10} + \frac{14}{10} \right|$.
Answer: Area is \(\frac{41}{20}\) sq. units (or \(2.05\) sq. units).
