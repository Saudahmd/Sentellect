1. Distance Formula (d)

Finding the Straight-Line Distance

The distance formula gives the straight-line distance between two points \(A(x_1,y_1)\) and \(B(x_2,y_2)\).

Distance Formula: \( d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \)
üìè Think of it as the hypotenuse of a right triangle!

Step 3: \( = \sqrt{50} = 5\sqrt{2} \)

2. Midpoint Formula (M)

The Midpoint ‚Äî The Perfect Center

The midpoint is the point exactly halfway between two endpoints. Take the average of x's and y's.

Midpoint Formula: \( M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \right) \)
üéØ Exactly halfway between two points!
A(2,4)
B(8,2)
M(5,3)
Example 1: A(3,1) & B(-2,-4)
Step 1: \( M = \left( \frac{3+(-2)}{2},\frac{1+(-4)}{2} \right) \)
Step 2: \( = \left( \frac{1}{2}, -\frac{3}{2} \right) \)
Example 2: A(-8,3) & B(2,-1)
Step 1: \( M = \left( \frac{-8+2}{2},\frac{3+(-1)}{2} \right) \)
Step 2: \( = (-3,1) \)

3. Section Formula (P)

Section Formula ‚Äî Dividing a Line Segment

Find the co-ordinates of a point that divides the line segment AB in the ratio m:n.

Section Formula: \( P = \left( \frac{m x_2 + n x_1}{m+n}, \frac{m y_2 + n y_1}{m+n} \right) \)

4. Collinearity of Points

Collinearity ‚Äî Points on the Same Line

Three points are collinear if they lie on the same straight line. Use slope or area test.

Collinearity Tests:
1. Slopes method: Slope AB = Slope BC.
2. Area method: Area of triangle ŒîABC = 0.
üìê If three points line up, they're collinear!
A(2,3)
B(5,4)
C(8,5)
Collinear!
Example: A(1,2), B(3,4), C(5,6)
Step 1: Slope AB = (4-2)/(3-1) = 1
Step 2: Slope BC = (6-4)/(5-3) = 1 ‚Üí slopes equal ‚Üí collinear
