1. Point of Intersection of Two Lines

Where Do They Meet? (Intersection)

This is the unique point (x,y) where two non-parallel lines cross each other. We solve their equations simultaneously. Think of it as finding the single spot on a map that belongs to two different roads!

Cross-Multiplication Formula: The solution to a‚ÇÅx+b‚ÇÅy+c‚ÇÅ=0 and a‚ÇÇx+b‚ÇÇy+c‚ÇÇ=0 is:

üõ£Ô∏è The intersection point satisfies both line equations.
Example 1: Integer Solution (Road Crossing)

Question: Find the point of intersection of: x-2y+1=0 and 2x-y+2=0

Step 1: Identify coefficients: a‚ÇÅ=1, b‚ÇÅ=-2, c‚ÇÅ=1 and a‚ÇÇ=2, b‚ÇÇ=-1, c‚ÇÇ=2.
Step 2: Calculate the denominator D = a‚ÇÅb‚ÇÇ-a‚ÇÇb‚ÇÅ = (1)(-1)-(2)(-2) = 3.
Step 3: Solve for x: x = (b‚ÇÅc‚ÇÇ-b‚ÇÇc‚ÇÅ)/D = ((-2)(2)-(-1)(1))/3 = -1.
Step 4: Solve for y: y = -(a‚ÇÅc‚ÇÇ-a‚ÇÇc‚ÇÅ)/D = -((1)(2)-(2)(1))/3 = 0.
Answer: The point of intersection is (-1, 0).
Example 2: Simple Fraction Solution

Question: Find the point of intersection of: 3x+y+12=0 and x+2y-1=0

Step 1: Identify coefficients: a‚ÇÅ=3, b‚ÇÅ=1, c‚ÇÅ=12 and a‚ÇÇ=1, b‚ÇÇ=2, c‚ÇÇ=-1.
Step 2: Calculate the denominator D = a‚ÇÅb‚ÇÇ-a‚ÇÇb‚ÇÅ = (3)(2)-(1)(1) = 5.
Step 3: Solve for x: x = ((1)(-1)-(2)(12))/5 = -5.

Answer: The point of intersection is (-5, 3).
Example 3: Line through Intersection (Family of Lines)

Question: Find the line through intersection of 2x+5y-8=0 and 3x-4y-6=0 that passes through (2,1).

Step 1: Use L‚ÇÅ + ŒªL‚ÇÇ = 0: (2x+5y-8) + Œª(3x-4y-6) = 0.

2. Three Concurrent Lines

The Three-Way Meetup (Concurrency)

Three lines are concurrent if they all pass through the exact same point. We check this by seeing if the determinant of their coefficients is zero.

Concurrency Condition (Determinant Test): For L‚ÇÅ, L‚ÇÇ, L‚ÇÉ:

| a‚ÇÅ b‚ÇÅ c‚ÇÅ |
| a‚ÇÇ b‚ÇÇ c‚ÇÇ | = 0
| a‚ÇÉ b‚ÇÉ c‚ÇÉ |

üéØ If the determinant is zero, all three lines hit the bullseye!
Example 1: Finding the unknown 'p'

Question: Find p if 2x+3y-1=0, 3x-y-5=0, and 3x+py+8=0 are concurrent.

Step 1: Set up the determinant = 0 using coefficients.
Step 2: Expand: 2(8 - (-5)p) - 3(24 - (-15)) + (-1)(3p - (-3)) = 0.
Step 3: Simplify: 16 - 10p - 117 - 3p - 3 = 0.
Step 4: Solve for p: -13p - 104 = 0.
Answer: The lines are concurrent when p = -8.
Example 2: Find Concurrency Point

Question: Find the point where 4x-3y-8=0, 3x-4y-6=0, and x-y-2=0 meet.

Step 1: Find intersection of first two lines (L‚ÇÅ, L‚ÇÇ): 4x-3y=8 and 3x-4y=6.
Step 2: Use elimination: 4 √ó L‚ÇÅ - 3 √ó L‚ÇÇ ‚áí 7x=14 ‚áí x=2.
Step 3: Substitute x=2 into L‚ÇÅ: 4(2)-3y=8 ‚áí y=0. Point is (2, 0).
Step 4: Check point (2, 0) in L‚ÇÉ: 2-0-2=0. It works, so they are concurrent.
Answer: The concurrency point is (2, 0).
Example 3: Non-Concurrent Check (Forms a Triangle)

Question: Check concurrency of 2x+3y+4=0, x-2y-3=0, 3x+y-8=0.

Step 1: Set up the determinant using coefficients.
Step 2: Expand: 2(16 - (-3)) - 3(-8 - (-9)) + 4(1 - (-6)).
Step 3: Calculate: 2(19) - 3(1) + 4(7) = 63.
Answer: Since the determinant is 63 ‚â† 0, the lines are not concurrent.

3. Angle Between Two Lines

How Sharp is the Turn? (Angle Formula)

We use the slopes (m‚ÇÅ and m‚ÇÇ) to find the acute angle (Œ∏) between two non-perpendicular lines. Remember, slope is key!

Angle Formula: Where Œ∏ is the acute angle:

4. Special Triangle Centres (The Core Builders)

Orthocentre, Circumcentre, and Centroid
