Distance Formula

Distance Between Two Points

Find how far apart two points are using the distance formula.

Distance Formula: \( d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \)
üß≠

Distance Concept Explained

The distance formula finds the straight-line distance between two points
It's based on the Pythagorean theorem - imagine a right triangle!
The horizontal and vertical differences form the triangle's legs
üìç
Example 1: A(3,1) and B(-2,-4)
Step 1: \( d = \sqrt{(-2-3)^2 + (-4-1)^2} \)
Step 2: \( = \sqrt{(-5)^2 + (-5)^2} = \sqrt{25 + 25} \)
Step 3: \( = \sqrt{50} = 5\sqrt{2} \)
üó∫Ô∏è
Example 2: A(-8,3) and B(2,-1)
Step 1: \( d = \sqrt{(2+8)^2 + (-1-3)^2} \)
Step 2: \( = \sqrt{(10)^2 + (-4)^2} = \sqrt{100 + 16} \)
Step 3: \( = \sqrt{116} = 2\sqrt{29} \)

Midpoint Formula

Finding the Midpoint

Find the point exactly halfway between two given points.

Midpoint Formula: \( M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \right) \)
‚öñÔ∏è

Midpoint Concept Explained

The midpoint is exactly halfway between two points
It's like finding the center point of a line segment
Average the x-coordinates and y-coordinates separately
ü§ù
Example 1: A(3,1) and B(-2,-4)
Step 1: \( M = \left( \frac{3 + (-2)}{2}, \frac{1 + (-4)}{2} \right) \)
Step 2: \( = \left( \frac{1}{2}, -\frac{3}{2} \right) \)
üéØ
Example 2: A(-8,3) and B(2,-1)
Step 1: \( M = \left( \frac{-8 + 2}{2}, \frac{3 + (-1)}{2} \right) \)
Step 2: \( = \left( \frac{-6}{2}, \frac{2}{2} \right) = (-3, 1) \)
Try It Yourself

1. Find midpoint of (0,0) and (4,4)

2. Find midpoint of (-2,-2) and (2,2)

‚öñÔ∏èüíñ
Perfectly balanced! You've found the center of that line segment.
Section Formula

Dividing a Line Segment

Find a point that divides a line segment in a given ratio.

Section Formula: \( P = \left( \frac{mx_2 + nx_1}{m+n}, \frac{my_2 + ny_1}{m+n} \right) \)
Where m:n is the ratio in which point divides the segment
üìè

Section Formula Concept Explained

The section formula finds a point that divides a line in a specific ratio
It's like cutting a rope into pieces of certain lengths
The m:n ratio tells how the segment is divided internally
üç´
Example 1: A(-1,4) and B(6,2) in ratio 1:2
Step 1: \( P = \left( \frac{1(6) + 2(-1)}{1+2}, \frac{1(2) + 2(4)}{1+2} \right) \)
Step 2: \( = \left( \frac{6 - 2}{3}, \frac{2 + 8}{3} \right) \)
Step 3: \( = \left( \frac{4}{3}, \frac{10}{3} \right) \)
üåâ
Example 2: A(-5,8) and B(5,3) in ratio 3:2
Step 1: \( P = \left( \frac{3(5) + 2(-5)}{3+2}, \frac{3(3) + 2(8)}{3+2} \right) \)
Step 2: \( = \left( \frac{15 - 10}{5}, \frac{9 + 16}{5} \right) \)
Step 3: \( = \left( 1, 5 \right) \)
Try It Yourself

1. Find point dividing (0,0) and (6,6) in ratio 1:2

2. Find point dividing (-2,-2) and (4,4) in ratio 2:1

Collinearity of Points

Checking if Points are Collinear

Determine if three points lie on the same straight line.

Method 1 (Slopes): Points are collinear if slopes between all pairs are equal.
Method 2 (Area): Points are collinear if area of triangle formed by them is zero.
üìê

Collinearity Concept Explained

Collinear points lie on the same straight line
Check if slopes between point pairs are equal
Or check if the triangle area formed by them is zero
üìø
Example 1: A(-1,1), B(3,2), C(7,3)
Step 1: Find slope AB = \( \frac{2-1}{3-(-1)} = \frac{1}{4} \)
Step 2: Find slope BC = \( \frac{3-2}{7-3} = \frac{1}{4} \)
Step 3: Since slopes are equal, points are collinear
üöÇ
Example 2: A(1,2), B(2,4), C(3,6)
Step 1: Find slope AB = \( \frac{4-2}{2-1} = 2 \)
Step 2: Find slope BC = \( \frac{6-4}{3-2} = 2 \)
Step 3: Since slopes are equal, points are collinear
