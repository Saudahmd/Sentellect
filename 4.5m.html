Understanding Homogeneous Equations

What are Homogeneous Equations?

Homogeneous equations are second-degree equations of the form \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \) that always represent a pair of straight lines passing through the origin (0,0).

Why Study Homogeneous Equations?

Homogeneous equations form the foundation for understanding:

‚Ä¢ Pair of straight lines through origin

‚Ä¢ Angle between lines

‚Ä¢ Perpendicular lines and their equations

‚Ä¢ Area calculations involving these lines

Visual Representation

Imagine two straight lines intersecting at the origin. The homogeneous equation represents both lines simultaneously. The coefficients determine their slopes and the angle between them.

This concept is used in many fields including physics, engineering, and computer graphics.

Working with Homogeneous Equations

What is a Homogeneous Equation?

A homogeneous equation of the second degree in x and y is of the form \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \). It always represents a pair of straight lines through the origin.

How to Factor Homogeneous Equations?

To find the separate lines, we factor the equation. For example:

\( 6x^2 - 5xy + y^2 = 0 \) can be factored as \( (2x - y)(3x - y) = 0 \)

This gives us two lines: \( 2x - y = 0 \) and \( 3x - y = 0 \)

Visual Representation

Imagine two straight lines crossing at the origin. The homogeneous equation contains both lines, and factoring reveals them.

This concept is used in geometry, physics, and engineering.

Line 2: 3x-y=0

Key Properties

‚Ä¢ Always passes through origin (0,0)

‚Ä¢ Can be factored into two linear equations

‚Ä¢ The product of slopes = a/b

‚Ä¢ The sum of slopes = -2h/b

Finding the Angle Between Lines

Learn how to calculate the angle between the two lines represented by a homogeneous equation.

Understanding Angle Between Lines

What is the Angle Between Lines?

The angle between the two lines represented by \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \) can be found using the formula:

\( \tan\theta = \frac{2\sqrt{h^2 - ab}}{a + b} \)

How to Calculate the Angle?

For the homogeneous equation \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \):

1. Identify coefficients a, h, b

2. Calculate \( h^2 - ab \)

3. Apply the formula: \( \tan\theta = \frac{2\sqrt{h^2 - ab}}{a + b} \)

4. Find Œ∏ using inverse tangent

Visual Representation

Imagine two straight lines crossing at the origin. The angle between them determines how "open" or "closed" they are relative to each other.

This concept is used in geometry, navigation, and engineering.

Example 1: Finding Angle Between Lines

Find the angle between the lines represented by \( 6x^2 - 5xy + y^2 = 0 \)

Step 1: Identify coefficients: a=6, h=-5/2, b=1
Step 2: Calculate h¬≤ - ab: \( (-5/2)^2 - (6)(1) = 25/4 - 6 = 1/4 \)
Step 3: Apply formula: \( \tan\theta = \frac{2\sqrt{1/4}}{6+1} = \frac{2(1/2)}{7} = \frac{1}{7} \)
Step 4: Find Œ∏: \( \theta = \tan^{-1}(1/7) \approx 8.13^\circ \)

Perpendicular Lines

Finding Perpendicular Lines

Learn how to find the equation of lines perpendicular to those represented by a homogeneous equation.

Understanding Perpendicular Lines

What are Perpendicular Lines?

Two lines are perpendicular if the angle between them is 90¬∞. For lines represented by \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \), the condition for perpendicularity is a + b = 0.

How to Find Perpendicular Lines?

If the homogeneous equation is \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \), then the equation of lines perpendicular to these is:

\( bx^2 - 2hxy + ay^2 = 0 \)

Visual Representation

Imagine two lines crossing at right angles. If you rotate each line by 90¬∞, you get new lines that are perpendicular to the original ones.

This concept is used in geometry, architecture, and engineering.

‚Ä¢ Perpendicular lines intersect at 90¬∞

‚Ä¢ For perpendicular lines, a + b = 0

‚Ä¢ The product of slopes is -1

‚Ä¢ The perpendicular equation swaps a and b, and changes sign of 2h

Perpendicular Lines: If \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \) represents two lines, then

Perpendicular Transformation

Finding perpendicular lines is like rotating the original lines by 90 degrees. The formula does this transformation mathematically!

üìç
Example 1: Finding Perpendicular Lines

Find the equation of lines perpendicular to those represented by \( 6x^2 - 5xy + y^2 = 0 \)

Step 1: Identify coefficients: a=6, h=-5/2, b=1
Step 2: Swap a and b: new a=1, new b=6
Step 3: Change sign of 2h: new 2h = 5
Step 4: Write new equation: \( x^2 + 5xy + 6y^2 = 0 \)
üó∫Ô∏è
Example 2: Perpendicular to Perpendicular Lines

Show that applying the perpendicular transformation twice gives back the original equation

Step 1: Start with: \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \)
Step 2: Apply perpendicular transformation: \( bx^2 - 2hxy + ay^2 = 0 \)
Step 3: Apply transformation again: \( ay^2 - 2(-h)xy + bx^2 = 0 \)
Step 4: Simplify: \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \) (original equation)
üßÆ
Example 3: Special Case - Already Perpendicular

Find the perpendicular lines to \( x^2 - y^2 = 0 \)

Step 1: Identify coefficients: a=1, h=0, b=-1
Step 2: Swap a and b: new a=-1, new b=1
Step 3: Change sign of 2h: new 2h = 0
Step 4: Write new equation: \( -x^2 + 0xy + y^2 = 0 \) or \( x^2 - y^2 = 0 \)
Step 5: The perpendicular lines are the same as the original lines (they're already perpendicular)

Area Calculation

Finding Area Bounded by Lines

Understanding Area Calculation

What is Area Calculation?

When a homogeneous equation \( ax^2 + 2hxy + by^2 = 0 \) is combined with another line, they form a triangle. We can calculate the area of this triangle using coordinate geometry formulas.

How to Calculate Area?

For a triangle with vertices (x‚ÇÅ, y‚ÇÅ), (x‚ÇÇ, y‚ÇÇ), (x‚ÇÉ, y‚ÇÉ), the area is:

\( \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} | x_1(y_2 - y_3) + x_2(y_3 - y_1) + x_3(y_1 - y_2) | \)

Visual Representation

Imagine two lines from the homogeneous equation intersecting at the origin, and a third line cutting across them to form a triangle. The area formula helps calculate the size of this triangle.

This concept is used in geometry, surveying, and computer graphics.

Example 1: Area of Triangle

Find the area of the triangle formed by the lines \( 6x^2 - 5xy + y^2 = 0 \) and \( x + 2y = 4 \)

Step 1: Factor the homogeneous equation: \( (2x - y)(3x - y) = 0 \)
Step 2: Find intersections with \( x + 2y = 4 \):
Step 3: Solve \( 2x - y = 0 \) and \( x + 2y = 4 \): Point A (4/5, 8/5)
Step 4: Solve \( 3x - y = 0 \) and \( x + 2y = 4 \): Point B (4/7, 12/7)
Step 5: Vertices: O(0,0), A(4/5, 8/5), B(4/7, 12/7)
Step 6: Apply area formula: Area = 1/2 | 0(8/5-12/7) + 4/5(12/7-0) + 4/7(0-8/5) |
Step 7: Calculate: Area = 1/2 | 0 + 48/35 - 32/35 | = 1/2 | 16/35 | = 8/35

Example 2: Area with Different Line

Find the area of the triangle formed by the lines \( 2x^2 + 3xy - 2y^2 = 0 \) and \( 2x + y = 6 \)

Step 1: Factor the homogeneous equation: \( (2x - y)(x + 2y) = 0 \)
Step 2: Find intersections with \( 2x + y = 6 \):
Step 3: Solve \( 2x - y = 0 \) and \( 2x + y = 6 \): Point A (3/2, 3)
Step 4: Solve \( x + 2y = 0 \) and \( 2x + y = 6 \): Point B (4, -2)
Step 5: Vertices: O(0,0), A(3/2, 3), B(4, -2)
Step 6: Apply area formula: Area = 1/2 | 0(3-(-2)) + 3/2(-2-0) + 4(0-3) |
Step 7: Calculate: Area = 1/2 | 0 - 3 - 12 | = 1/2 | -15 | = 15/2

Example 3: Special Case - Zero Area

Find the area of the triangle formed by the lines \( x^2 - y^2 = 0 \) and \( x + y = 0 \)

Step 1: Factor the homogeneous equation: \( (x - y)(x + y) = 0 \)
Step 2: Find intersections with \( x + y = 0 \):
Step 3: Solve \( x - y = 0 \) and \( x + y = 0 \): Point A (0,0)
Step 4: Solve \( x + y = 0 \) and \( x + y = 0 \): All points on the line
Step 5: The three lines are concurrent (all pass through origin)
Step 6: No triangle is formed, area = 0
