Distance Formula

Distance Between Two Points

Find how far apart two points are using the distance formula.

Understanding Distance Formula

What is the Distance Formula?

The distance formula calculates the straight-line distance between two points in a coordinate plane. It's derived from the Pythagorean theorem, which relates the sides of a right triangle.

How Does the Distance Formula Work?

When we have two points A(x‚ÇÅ,y‚ÇÅ) and B(x‚ÇÇ,y‚ÇÇ), the distance between them is given by:

\[ d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \]

This formula comes from creating a right triangle with the two points and finding the length of the hypotenuse.

Visual Representation

Imagine two points on a map. The distance formula gives you the straight-line distance between them, like a bird would fly from one point to the other.

This is useful in many real-world applications like navigation, game development, and physics.

Midpoint Formula

Finding the Midpoint

Find the point exactly halfway between two given points.

Understanding Midpoint Formula

What is the Midpoint Formula?

The midpoint formula finds the exact center point between two given points in a coordinate plane. This point divides the line segment joining the two points into two equal parts.

How Does the Midpoint Formula Work?

When we have two points A(x‚ÇÅ,y‚ÇÅ) and B(x‚ÇÇ,y‚ÇÇ), the midpoint M is given by:

\[ M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \right) \]

This formula essentially averages the x-coordinates and y-coordinates separately to find the center.

Visual Representation

Imagine you have two friends who want to meet halfway between their houses. The midpoint formula gives you the exact location where they should meet.

This concept is used in many fields including computer graphics, navigation, and architecture.

Section Formula

Dividing a Line Segment

Find a point that divides a line segment in a given ratio.

Understanding Section Formula

What is the Section Formula?

The section formula finds a point that divides a line segment joining two points in a given ratio. This is a generalization of the midpoint formula.

How Does the Section Formula Work?

When we have two points A(x‚ÇÅ,y‚ÇÅ) and B(x‚ÇÇ,y‚ÇÇ), and we want to find point P that divides AB in the ratio m:n, the coordinates of P are:

\[ P = \left( \frac{mx_2 + nx_1}{m+n}, \frac{my_2 + ny_1}{m+n} \right) \]

When m:n = 1:1, this reduces to the midpoint formula.

Visual Representation

Imagine you have a chocolate bar and you want to divide it between two friends in a specific ratio. The section formula tells you exactly where to make the cut.

This concept is used in physics for finding centers of mass, in computer graphics for interpolation, and in many other applications.

Collinearity of Points

Checking if Points are Collinear

Determine if three points lie on the same straight line.

Understanding Collinearity

What is Collinearity?

Collinearity refers to points that lie on the same straight line. In coordinate geometry, we have methods to check if three or more points are collinear.

How to Check for Collinearity?

There are two main methods to check if points A, B, and C are collinear:

Method 1 (Slopes): Points are collinear if slopes between all pairs are equal.

Method 2 (Area): Points are collinear if area of triangle formed by them is zero.

Visual Representation

Imagine beads on a string. If all beads lie on the same straight string, they are collinear. If even one bead is off the string, they're not collinear.

This concept is important in geometry proofs, computer graphics, and many real-world applications.

Step 3: Since slopes are equal, points are collinear

Step 1: Find slope AB = \( \frac{2-0}{2-0} = 1 \)
Step 2: Find slope BC = \( \frac{0-2}{2-2} = \frac{-2}{0} \) (undefined)
Step 3: Since slopes are different, points are not collinear
